How the score works

Brier-graded against real outcomes.

Every claim is scored against what actually happened: a confident “here we go” on a deal that collapsed is punished hard; a hedged “in talks” on the same deal barely dents you. Scores are shrunk toward the field average by sample size (K=20) so a few lucky calls can’t top the table, and breaking a deal genuinely early earns a small bonus. One honest caveat: mid-window, completed deals resolve faster than collapses (a collapse often needs the window to close), so standings can tilt toward confident callers until deadline day grades the misses.
